# The Secret Sauce of Great Design Systems

**TheGeekConf Berlin — July 2024, Berlin, Germany**

At TheGeekConf Berlin, I spoke about what makes a design system truly effective—not from the outside, but from the developer’s seat. The talk focused on how Developer Experience (DX) directly impacts adoption, scalability, and longevity of design libraries in React and React Native ecosystems.

## Talk Overview

Design libraries are judged not only by their aesthetics but by how easily developers can work with them. This talk unpacked several layers of what makes or breaks that experience.

**Topics Covered**

- Common DX pitfalls that stall adoption of otherwise well-designed libraries
- How semantics, consistency, and standardization influence developer confidence
- A breakdown of primitives vs abstractions and how to plan for flexibility without adding clutter
- Purpose-driven design workflows with the "Three W’s and an H" approach
- Tips for scaling DX through tooling—CLI scaffolding, customization hooks, and better defaults
- Importance of documentation, accessibility, community engagement, and feedback loops

## Key Points

- The structure and ergonomics of a design library are as important as visual design
- Clear semantics and strict consistency drive faster team onboarding and lower maintenance
- Libraries must plan for long-term extensibility without overwhelming the base API
- Supporting DX through tooling and versioned documentation increases developer trust
